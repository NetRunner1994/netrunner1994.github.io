🖥️ Graphics Card (GPU) KEY PART
The GPU is the heart of gaming and visual work. It's what pushes pixels to your screen. More VRAM = more detail, higher resolution, better performance in modern games.
Select a budget above to see GPU options.
⚙️ Processor (CPU)
The CPU handles game logic, background apps, and everything your GPU doesn't. Think of it as the brain. More cores = smoother multitasking and better performance in CPU-heavy games.
Select a budget above to see CPU options.
🔌 Motherboard REQUIRED
The motherboard connects every component together. It has to match your CPU's socket — AM4, AM5, LGA1700, or LGA1851. Chipset (B650, X670, Z790, etc.) determines features, overclocking support, and expansion slots.
Select a budget above to see motherboard options.
🧠 Memory (RAM)
RAM is your PC's short-term workspace — the more you have, the more your system can juggle at once. 16GB handles most games. 32GB is the new sweet spot. 64GB+ for serious workloads.
Select a budget above to see RAM options.
💾 Storage (SSD)
Real brand names matter here — they affect speed, reliability, and lifespan. Samsung and WD are the gold standards. Kingston NV3 is solid budget. Avoid no-name drives for your main OS/game drive.
🏠 Case (Chassis)
The case is the body of your PC. It affects airflow, size, and aesthetics. Mid-towers are the sweet spot — plenty of room for components and great cooling. Want to show off your build? Go glass panel with RGB.
